<p><p>Biochips are a type of microarray technology that have various applications in the field of biotechnology. They consist of a substrate on which tiny probes are immobilized for detecting specific biological molecules. DNA chips are biochips used for analyzing genetic information by detecting variations in DNA sequences. Lab-on-a-chip is a biochip that integrates multiple laboratory functions on a single chip, enabling tasks like sample analysis and separation to be performed in a small device. Protein chips are used for studying protein interactions, while other types of biochips are designed for specific applications such as drug discovery or diagnostic testing.</p></p>
