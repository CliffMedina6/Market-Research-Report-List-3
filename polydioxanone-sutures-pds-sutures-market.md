<p><p>Polydioxanone sutures, commonly known as PDS sutures, are synthetic absorbable sutures used in various surgical procedures. The increasing adoption of these sutures due to their superior qualities such as high tensile strength, prolonged tensile holding, and low tissue reactivity is primarily driving the market growth. Additionally, the rising number of surgical procedures, including cardiovascular, orthopedic, and cosmetic surgeries, is further fueling the demand for PDS sutures.</p><p>Moreover, the growing geriatric population and the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases are boosting the usage of PDS sutures in medical procedures. These sutures are also preferred by surgeons as they provide excellent knot security and minimize the risk of infection and scarring. Furthermore, technological advancements in suture materials and increasing investments in research and development activities are expected to drive market growth.</p><p>Several latest trends are observed in the PDS sutures market. The introduction of barbed sutures, which eliminate the need for knot tying, is gaining popularity among surgeons due to their time-saving and cost-effective nature.
