<p><p>Run-flat tires, also known as run-flat tires, are designed to prevent a complete loss of air pressure in the event of a puncture, allowing the vehicle to continue driving for a limited distance until the tire can be safely replaced. There are two main types of run-flat tire systems: self-supporting and support ring systems. Self-supporting run-flat tires have reinforced sidewalls that support the vehicle's weight even when there is no air pressure, while support ring systems use a rigid ring inside the tire to provide support in case of a puncture.</p></p>
