<p><p>The Veterinary Diagnostic Imaging market encompasses various types of imaging techniques used in veterinary medicine. X-ray imaging uses electromagnetic radiation to create images of bones and tissues. Ultrasound employs sound waves to produce images of organs and soft tissues. MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) uses magnetic fields and radio waves to generate detailed images of the body. Other imaging techniques may include CT (Computed Tomography) scans, endoscopy, and nuclear imaging. These diagnostic tools aid in the detection and diagnosis of diseases, injuries, and abnormalities in animals, facilitating effective veterinary care.</p></p>
