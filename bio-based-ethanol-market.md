<p><p>Bio-based ethanol is a type of renewable fuel that is derived from biomass, such as corn or cellulose. Corn-based ethanol is the most commonly produced type, made from fermenting corn starch. Cellulosic ethanol, on the other hand, is produced from agricultural residues or wood chips through a more complex process that breaks down cellulose into sugars. Both types of ethanol are considered more environmentally-friendly alternatives to traditional gasoline, as they produce lower emissions and reduce dependence on fossil fuels.</p></p>
