<p><p>Anesthesia breathing circuits are used in medical procedures to deliver anesthesia gases and oxygen to patients. There are two types of anesthesia breathing circuits: non-rebreathing circuits and rebreathing circuits. Non-rebreathing circuits are designed to deliver fresh gases to the patient without reusing exhaled gases. This reduces the risk of contamination. Rebreathing circuits, on the other hand, recirculate and filter exhaled gases, allowing for a more efficient use of anesthesia gases. Both types of circuits have their benefits and are used based on the specific needs of the patient and procedure.</p></p>
