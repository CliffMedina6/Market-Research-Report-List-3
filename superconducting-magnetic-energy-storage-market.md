Low-Temperature Superconductors, such as niobium-titanium, operate at very low temperatures close to absolute zero, while High-Temperature Superconductors, such as yttrium barium copper oxide, can operate at higher temperatures closer to -196 degrees Celsius. Each type has its advantages and applications, with High-Temperature Superconductors offering the potential for more practical and cost-effective energy storage solutions.</p></p>
