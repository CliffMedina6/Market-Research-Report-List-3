<p><ul><li>Dynamic Orthotics</li><li>Static Orthotics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Adult spinal orthoses are devices used to support and stabilize the spine in individuals with spinal conditions or injuries. Dynamic orthotics are orthoses that have moving parts or components, allowing for controlled movement of the spine. They provide flexibility and enable mobility while still providing support. On the other hand, static orthotics are non-adjustable devices that immobilize and restrict movement of the spine. They are primarily used for individuals with severe spinal injuries or conditions that require complete immobilization. Both types of orthotics play an important role in providing necessary support and stabilization for individuals with spinal issues.</p></p>
