<p><p>Breast reconstruction is a surgical procedure that recreates the shape, size, and appearance of breasts after mastectomy. Implant reconstruction involves inserting silicone or saline implants to restore breast volume and shape. Autologous reconstruction, also known as flap reconstruction, uses the patient's own tissue, like muscle, fat, or skin, from another area of the body, such as the abdomen or back, to rebuild the breast. Both methods aim to give women a sense of normalcy and confidence after breast cancer surgery.</p></p>
