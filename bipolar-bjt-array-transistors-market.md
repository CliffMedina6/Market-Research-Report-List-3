<p><p>Bipolar (BJT) Array Transistors are available in two main types - NPN and PNP. NPN transistors are constructed with a layer of P-type semiconductor between two layers of N-type semiconductor, while PNP transistors have a layer of N-type semiconductor between two layers of P-type semiconductor. NPN transistors are commonly used for amplification and switching applications, while PNP transistors are frequently used in complementary circuits. Both types play a crucial role in electronic devices and circuits by allowing for controlled amplification and switching of electrical signals.</p></p>
