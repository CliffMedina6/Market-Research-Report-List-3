<p><p>The DNA purification market comprises two types: Chemical Methods and Biotechnology. Chemical Methods involve the use of reagents and kits to isolate DNA from a sample. These methods are cost-effective but may have limitations in terms of purity and yield. On the other hand, Biotechnology methods use advanced techniques like column chromatography and magnetic beads to ensure high-quality DNA purification. Although more expensive, biotechnology methods offer better purity, yield, and compatibility for downstream applications. Both types cater to the growing demand for DNA purification in research, diagnostics, and forensic applications.</p></p>
