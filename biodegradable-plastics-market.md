<p><ul><li>PLA</li><li>Starch Blends</li><li>PCL</li><li>Regenerated Cellulose</li><li>PBS</li><li>PHA</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Biodegradable plastics market can be categorized into various types, including PLA, Starch Blends, PCL, Regenerated Cellulose, PBS, and PHA. Polylactic acid (PLA) is derived from renewable resources, while Starch Blends are made from a mixture of starch and biodegradable polymers. Poly-caprolactone (PCL) is known for its flexibility and durability. Regenerated Cellulose is made from plant fibers. Polybutylene succinate (PBS) is biodegradable and compostable. Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) are produced by microorganisms and are fully biodegradable, making them environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional plastics.</p></p>
