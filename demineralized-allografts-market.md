<p><p>Demineralized allografts are bone graft materials derived from human donors and processed to remove minerals while retaining the organic components of the bone. There are three main types available in the market: gel, putty, and putty with chips. Gel is a form of demineralized allograft that has a gel-like consistency, making it easy to handle and mold. Putty is a more solid form that can be shaped and packed into bone voids or defects. Putty with chips combines the putty form with small chips or granules for added stability and support during bone regeneration procedures.</p></p>
