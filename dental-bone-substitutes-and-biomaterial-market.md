<p><p>The dental bone substitutes and biomaterial market comprises four main types: natural, ceramics, composites, and polymers. Natural bone substitutes are derived from human, animal, or plant sources and mimic natural bone properties. Ceramics are biocompatible materials like hydroxyapatite that promote bone regeneration. Composites consist of two or more materials, often including ceramics and polymers, to enhance the mechanical properties of bone fillers. Polymers are synthetic materials that can be customized for specific dental applications, providing flexibility and stability in bone reconstruction.</p></p>
