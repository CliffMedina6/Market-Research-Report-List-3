<p><ul><li>Crystalloids</li><li>Colloids</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The intravenous solutions market is divided into three main types: crystalloids, colloids, and others. Crystalloids are solutions that contain small molecules and electrolytes, such as saline or lactated Ringer's solution. These solutions are effective in replenishing fluids and electrolytes in the body. Colloids, on the other hand, contain larger molecules, such as proteins or starches, which help to increase blood volume by exerting osmotic pressure. The "others" category includes specialized solutions like blood products or parenteral nutrition solutions, which are used for specific medical needs.</p></p>
