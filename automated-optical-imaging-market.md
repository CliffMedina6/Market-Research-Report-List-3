<p><p>Automated Optical Imaging refers to the use of advanced technologies for capturing and analyzing images of a biological sample without human intervention. Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a type of automated optical imaging that uses light waves to capture detailed cross-sectional images of tissues. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is another form of automated optical imaging that uses magnetic fields and radio waves to generate highly detailed images of the body's internal structures. Both OCT and MRI play crucial roles in medical diagnostics and research, enabling early disease detection and effective treatment planning.</p></p>
