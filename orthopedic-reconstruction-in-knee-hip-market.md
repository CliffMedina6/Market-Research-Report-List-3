<p><p>Orthopedic reconstruction in the knee and hip market includes various types such as Posterior Stabilized (PS) Fixed-bearing, Cruciate-retaining (CR) Fixed-bearing, Mobile-bearing, Unicompartmental, Knee Revision, and others. The Posterior Stabilized (PS) Fixed-bearing design provides stability by replacing both knee ligaments, while the Cruciate-retaining (CR) Fixed-bearing design preserves the natural knee ligaments. Mobile-bearing designs allow for improved mobility, while Unicompartmental implants replace only one part of the knee. Knee Revision procedures involve replacing a failed knee implant, and other options may include customized implants or patient-specific instrumentation.</p></p>
