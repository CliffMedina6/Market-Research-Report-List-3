<p><p>Medical radiation detection, monitoring, and safety market types include gas-filled detectors, Geiger Muller, and solid-state detectors. Gas-filled detectors utilize a gas-filled chamber where radiation ionizes the gas, producing a detectable signal. Geiger Muller detectors consist of a gas-filled tube with an electrode that detects radiation through the amplification of ionization events. Solid-state detectors employ semiconductor materials that directly detect radiation-induced charge, offering high accuracy and sensitivity. These types of detectors play a crucial role in ensuring radiation safety in medical settings, enabling accurate monitoring and protection for healthcare professionals and patients.</p></p>
