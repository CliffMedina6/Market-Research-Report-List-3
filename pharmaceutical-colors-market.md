<p><p>Pharmaceutical colors are commonly categorized into three types: synthetic, natural, and natural-identical. Synthetic colors are chemically produced additives that provide a wide range of vibrant colors. Natural colors are derived from natural sources such as fruits, vegetables, and minerals. Natural-identical colors are chemically identical to natural colors but are produced synthetically to ensure consistency and purity. Each market type offers different benefits and considerations, such as cost-effectiveness, sustainability, and regulatory compliance. Pharmaceutical companies choose colors based on their specific needs and requirements.</p></p>
