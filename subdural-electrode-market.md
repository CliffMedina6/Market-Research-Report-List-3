<p><p>A subdural electrode is a medical device that is used to record electrical activity in the brain. It consists of a thin sheet of electrode contacts that are placed directly on the surface of the brain, under the dura mater. These electrodes are used to monitor brain activity, identify the source of epileptic seizures, and assist in the planning of epilepsy surgery.</p><p>The global subdural electrode market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. Factors such as the increasing prevalence of epilepsy and other neurological disorders, advancements in electrode technologies, and rising demand for accurate diagnostic techniques are driving the market growth.</p><p>There is a growing trend towards the use of minimally invasive procedures in neurological surgeries, which is fueling the adoption of subdural electrodes. These electrodes offer numerous advantages over traditional invasive methods, including improved accuracy, reduced risks of infection, and faster recovery times. As a result, there is a rising demand for subdural electrodes in the market.</p><p>Additionally, technological advancements in subdural electrode designs are also contributing to market growth.
