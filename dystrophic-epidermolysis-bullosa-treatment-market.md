<p><p>Dystrophic Epidermolysis Bullosa (DEB) is a rare and genetic skin disorder characterized by the formation of blisters and erosions on the skin and mucous membranes. Wound care typically involves gentle cleansing, bandaging, and the use of topical ointments to promote healing and prevent infection. Pain management may include the use of medications and non-pharmacological interventions such as distraction techniques or relaxation exercises. Nutritional support is important to ensure adequate intake of nutrients for wound healing and overall health.</p><p>In recent years, there have been advancements in the treatment of DEB, including gene and cell therapy approaches.
